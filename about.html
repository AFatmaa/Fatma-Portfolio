About Me

This is the slightly longer version of how I found my way into software.

A SMALL CLUE

I studied chemistry at university, where one of my classes introduced me to basic programming. I loved it, but at the time I saw it as an interesting class, not the beginning of a career.

It took me a while to recognise the clue.

WORKING CLOSE TO PROBLEMS

I later spent several years working across finance, operations, international trade, production, and project management.

I used business software every day. Whenever a process felt unnecessarily complicated, I found myself wondering how the system worked and how it could work better. I especially enjoyed discussing those ideas with developers.

That probably should have been another clue.

STARTING AGAIN

After moving to London, I met people who had moved into technology from completely different careers. For the first time, becoming a developer stopped feeling like something meant for somebody else.

So I started learning.

LEARNING BY BUILDING

My training at CodeYourFuture was not only about completing lessons. I worked through a multi-stage programme built around projects, teamwork, pull requests, code reviews, testing, debugging, and explaining technical decisions.

I built applications using JavaScript, React, Node.js, Express, PostgreSQL, Python, and WebSockets. Some worked quickly. Others made me stare at the same bug for far longer than I would like to admit. Both kinds taught me something.

WHAT I DO NOW

Today, I build web applications and keep developing my understanding of how software works.

My previous career still shapes the way I approach code. I think about the person using the product, the problem behind the request, and whether the solution will be practical outside the happy path.

WHAT COMES NEXT

I’m looking for my first software development role in a team where I can contribute, ask good questions, learn from experienced developers, and take responsibility as I grow.

If that sounds like a conversation worth having, I’d be happy to hear from you.
